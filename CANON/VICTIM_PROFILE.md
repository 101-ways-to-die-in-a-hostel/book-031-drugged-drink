# Victim Profile

## Basic Information

**Full Name:** Ingrid Larsen
**Nickname/Handle:** @NordicNomadIngrid
**Age:** 28
**Nationality:** Norwegian
**Occupation:** HR Coordinator (on sabbatical)

## Archetype
**Primary:** Solo Traveler

**Secondary traits:** Over-trusting empath, serial hostel socializer, "yes and" personality

## The Fatal Flaw
**Core Flaw:** Naivety

**How it manifests:**
- Believes everyone she meets traveling shares her good intentions
- Takes people at face value, especially charming ones
- Ignores red flags because she doesn't want to seem judgmental or paranoid
- Has a deep need to connect and be liked

**How it kills them:**
Her desperate desire for connection makes her accept a drink from the wrong person, and her naivety prevents her from recognizing predatory behavior until it's too late.

## Physical Appearance
- **Height/Build:** 5'8", athletic Nordic build from years of hiking and skiing
- **Hair:** Blonde, usually in a practical braid or messy bun
- **Distinguishing features:** Light freckles across nose, warm blue eyes that make constant eye contact, infectious smile
- **Typical outfit:** Linen pants, simple tank top, Fjallraven daypack never far away, wooden bead bracelet from a "shaman" in Peru

## Personality

### Positive Traits (that get twisted)
1. Openness - becomes inability to set boundaries
2. Empathy - becomes vulnerability to manipulation
3. Optimism - becomes dangerous denial of warning signs

### Negative Traits (that seal their fate)
1. People-pleasing - leads to accepting a drink she didn't want
2. Fear of seeming rude - leads to staying in an uncomfortable situation

### Pet Peeves
- "Jaded" travelers who don't trust anyone
- People who stay in their dorms instead of socializing
- Closed-mindedness

### Fears
- Being alone forever
- Missing out on authentic connections
- Becoming cynical like her mother

## Background

### Origin Story
Grew up in Trondheim, Norway, in a upper-middle-class family where politeness was paramount. Her mother was emotionally distant and suspicious of everyone, which Ingrid rebelled against by becoming aggressively trusting. Worked in HR for five years, helping everyone else with their problems while ignoring her own loneliness.

### Why They Travel
Seeking the meaningful connections she couldn't find in her structured Norwegian life. Secretly hopes to meet someone - friend or romantic partner - who truly "gets" her. Running from the realization that she's 28 and has no close friends.

### How They Ended Up at This Hostel
Following the Southeast Asia backpacker trail, heard from a guy in Vietnam that this hostel in Phnom Penh had an amazing bar scene and the "best people." Booked immediately.

## Voice & Speech

### Vocabulary
- Uses words like: "authentic," "connection," "vibe," "energy," "the universe," "meant to be"
- Avoids words like: "suspicious," "weird," "sketchy," "no"
- Catchphrases: "I just feel like we were supposed to meet," "I'm such an empath"

### Speech Pattern
- Asks lots of follow-up questions to show she's listening
- Mirrors other people's speech patterns unconsciously
- Slightly formal English with occasional Norwegian syntax
- Laughs too readily at others' jokes

### Internal Monologue Style
Constantly analyzing interactions for deeper meaning, searching for signs of connection. Anxious undertone beneath performative confidence.

## Digital Presence

### Social Media
- **Platform of choice:** Instagram
- **Follower count:** 2,847
- **Posting style:** Philosophical captions about connection and growth, scenic shots, "candid" hostel social moments
- **Last post before death:** Photo of the hostel bar with caption "Tonight feels like the beginning of something. The universe brought me here for a reason."

### Online vs Reality
Curates an image of a confident solo traveler embracing adventure. In reality, cries in hostel bathrooms from loneliness and constantly seeks validation from strangers.

## Relationships

### Family
- Distant relationship with cold, suspicious mother (would say "I told you so" about traveling alone)
- Closer to her father, but he enables her naivety by always seeing the best in her

### Friends
- Many acquaintances, no true close friends
- Travel "friends" she's met along the way who never text back

### Romantic
- Single, desperately wants to change that
- History of falling fast for unavailable or inappropriate people

### At the Hostel
- Quickly befriends everyone in the common area
- Especially drawn to Damien, the charming British man who pays her special attention
- Other guests find her slightly exhausting but harmless

## The Day They Die

### Morning
Woke up excited about the "deep conversation" she had with Damien at the bar last night. Journaled about feeling like she'd finally met someone who understood her.

### Their Last Normal Moment
Sitting at the bar, laughing at Damien's story about teaching English in Thailand, feeling genuinely happy and seen for the first time in months.

### Final Thoughts
Confusion and betrayal as she realizes the paralysis creeping through her body isn't alcohol. A desperate, heartbroken "why?" as she understands Damien was never her friend.

### Last Words
"Damien, I don't... feel... right..."

## Writing This Character

### POV Notes
When in this character's head:
- They notice: Every smile directed at them, body language that suggests openness, opportunities to connect
- They ignore: Inconsistencies in stories, pushy behavior, the drink left unattended
- They judge: "Closed-off" travelers harshly, sees their own openness as superior

### Key Scenes Checklist
- [ ] Introduction scene establishing archetype - Ingrid making friends within minutes of checking in
- [ ] Fatal flaw demonstration (early) - ignoring a warning from another traveler about being too trusting
- [ ] Sympathetic moment (make reader care) - phone call with her father where she admits she's lonely
- [ ] Warning ignored scene - another guest hints that Damien seems "off"
- [ ] Point of no return - accepting the drink she didn't see poured
- [ ] The death

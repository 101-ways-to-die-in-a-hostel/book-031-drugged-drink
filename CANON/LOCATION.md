# Location Profile

## The Hostel

**Name:** The Wanderer's Rest
**Type:** Party / Social
**Star Rating:** 3.5
**Price Range:** $8-15 per night

### History
Originally a 1960s French colonial residence, converted to a guesthouse in the 1990s and rebranded as a backpacker hostel in 2010. Known for its legendary bar and social scene. Has had three suspicious deaths in the past decade, all ruled accidental. The original owner sold after his wife died under unclear circumstances; current owner is an Australian expat who asks few questions.

### Architecture
- **Style:** French colonial with backpacker modifications - original tile floors, shuttered windows, but cheap bunk beds and graffiti-covered walls added
- **Floors:** 3
- **Total beds:** 68
- **Notable features:** Central courtyard bar, original wrought-iron balconies, a mural of backpackers that new guests add to

### Layout

### The Death Location (Specific)
**Where exactly does the death occur?**
8-bed mixed dorm on the second floor, back corner of the hostel. Room 2B. Ingrid is in bed 7, a top bunk near the window. AC unit rattles constantly. The room has lockers but half are broken. Thin mattress, polyester sheets, a small reading light that doesn't work. The window overlooks the back alley where motorbikes are stored.

## The City

**City:** Phnom Penh
**Country:** Cambodia
**Population:** ~2.3 million
**Known for:** Killing Fields history, riverfront nightlife, temples, dark tourism, cheap living, expat party scene

### Geography
- **Climate:** Tropical monsoon, hot and humid year-round
- **Terrain:** Flat river city at confluence of Mekong and Tonle Sap

### Culture
- **Language(s):** Khmer (English widely spoken in tourist areas)
- **Religion(s):** Theravada Buddhism
- **Customs relevant to story:** Strong culture of saving face - locals unlikely to intervene in tourist business, bribes common to make problems disappear

- **Backpacker popularity:** High - major stop on Southeast Asia circuit

## Sensory Environment

### Sounds
- Morning: Monks chanting from nearby wat, motorbikes starting, roosters, the call of "soup, soup" from street vendors
- Afternoon: AC units humming, tuk-tuks honking, construction noise, tourists negotiating
- Night: Bass from the bar, drunk conversations in multiple languages, distant karaoke, motorbikes, the occasional scream of delight from the pool table
- Unique sounds: The particular rattle of the hostel's ancient AC, the creak of the colonial stairs

### Smells
- Hostel: Cheap disinfectant barely masking mold, stale beer from the bar, someone cooking instant noodles, sunscreen
- Street: Prahok (fermented fish), exhaust fumes, frangipani flowers, open sewers, grilling meat
- Specific to death location: Musty dorm smell, Damien's cologne lingering, the chemical edge of the drugged drink

### Textures
- Surfaces: Sticky bar top, gritty tile floors, peeling paint, rough cotton sheets
- Climate feel: Oppressive humidity that makes everything slightly damp, sweat that never dries

### Tastes
- Local food: Fish amok, lok lak, num banh chok, fresh fruit smoothies, Angkor beer
- Hostel offerings: Cheap beer ($0.50), basic fried rice, toast and jam breakfast

### Visual Details
- Colors: Faded colonial yellows and blues, bright backpacker graffiti, green mold on walls, neon beer signs
- Light quality: Harsh tropical sun during day, warm yellow bar lights at night, dim fluorescents in dorms
- Distinctive visuals: The ever-growing mural of travelers, Buddha statues repurposed as ashtrays, strings of Christmas lights year-round

## Hostel Atmosphere

### Day Vibe
Hungover and lazy. Guests sleep until noon, lounge in hammocks, plan temple visits they won't take. Kitchen busy with instant noodles and toast. A few ambitious souls leave early for Killing Fields tours.

### Night Vibe
Transforms completely. The Pit fills up by 8 PM. Cheap beer flows. Travelers compete at pool. Connections are made - some genuine, some predatory. Music gets louder until midnight. The desperate energy of people trying to have the time of their lives.

### Guest Demographics
Mix of:
- Gap year Brits and Aussies
- European backpackers on the circuit
- American study abroad students
- Digital nomads testing the lifestyle
- Older solo travelers escaping something
- Occasional sex tourists (staff tries to discourage)

### Reputation
4.1 stars on HostelWorld - "Amazing bar, great people, rooms are basic but who cares!" Whispered warnings on forums about petty theft and getting "too wasted." Beloved by party travelers, avoided by those seeking peace.

## Practical Details

### Getting There
Tuk-tuk from airport or bus station direct to hostel. Driver knows it by name. Located in the BKK1 area, walking distance to the riverfront.

### Check-in Process
Minimal - passport photocopy, cash payment, handed a key card that doesn't always work. Quick explanation of bar hours and checkout time. No safety briefing.

### Rules
- No outside guests after 10 PM (rarely enforced)
- Quiet hours 11 PM - 8 AM (never enforced in bar-adjacent dorms)
- No drugs (openly violated, staff looks away)
- Don't vomit in the pool (the only rule enforced)

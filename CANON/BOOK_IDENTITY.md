# Book Identity

## Core Information

**Book Number:** 031
**Title:** Last Call at the Wanderer's Rest
**Slug:** drugged-drink
**Category:** Fellow Travelers

## The Death (One Sentence)
A trusting solo traveler accepts a spiked drink from a charming stranger at the hostel bar and dies from a fatal drug overdose while being robbed.

## Tagline
"Never accept drinks from strangers. Especially friendly ones."

## Themes
1. Misplaced trust in fellow travelers - satirizes the naive belief that backpackers are inherently good people
2. The predatory underbelly of hostel social scenes
3. The dangerous desire to belong and connect while traveling alone

## Tone Balance
- Comedy: 25%
- Horror: 50%
- Satire: 25%

## Target Reader
Solo travelers who pride themselves on reading people, anyone who has accepted a drink from a stranger while traveling, those who romanticize hostel social life.

## Comparison Titles
- Hostel (2005) meets A Simple Favor - drugging and deception in seemingly safe social spaces

## Content Warnings
- Drug use and overdose
- Sexual predation themes (non-explicit)
- Robbery and victimization
- Solo traveler vulnerability
